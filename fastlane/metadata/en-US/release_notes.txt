PeerDrop 3.4.0 — Security Hardening

New
- First-contact verification: when a peer messages you for the first time, you'll be asked to compare a fingerprint before establishing an encrypted session — preventing silent man-in-the-middle attacks.
- Decryption alerts: if PeerDrop can't decrypt three messages in a row from the same peer (usually because their identity key changed), a banner now tells you to verify their fingerprint instead of failing silently.

Under the hood
- One-time pre-keys are now persisted as consumed before being removed, so a crash mid-handshake can't re-expose them for replay.
- Every identity-key rotation is recorded in a per-contact audit trail, so post-incident forensics can reconstruct what happened.
- Hardened the message reassembly path against malformed peers: caps message size at 256 MB, rejects out-of-range chunk indices, and detects conflicting duplicate chunks.
